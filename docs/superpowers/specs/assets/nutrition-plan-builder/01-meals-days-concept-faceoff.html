Schedule-centric (X) vs Two-section (Y)

Five things a coach does. Verdict each, tally, recommendation. Tap the winner.

1 · Build a standard plan (same meals daily) X

The 90% case — fill the day once.

X · schedule-centric win

Fill 6 slots in place. Daily template applies to all days. One pass, one list.

Y · two-section

Build 6 meals in the library, then go to schedule and assign each. Two passes.

2 · Same meal every day tie

Identical breakfast Mon–Sun.

X tie

Daily template already = same every day. Native, zero effort.

Y tie

Library meal assigned to each day. Also fine.

Note: X's daily template solves the most common reuse for free — no library needed.

3 · Reuse one meal across different slots Y

Casein shake as both PM snack and evening; pull a past meal in.

X · schedule-centric

"↻ Reuse in another slot" / pick from the meal list in the picker. One extra tap.

Y · two-section win

Library is the reuse surface — drag/assign the same meal anywhere, see all meals at once.

4 · Scan / manage every meal in the plan Y

"What meals exist? Rename, dedupe, clean up."

X · schedule-centric

Meals seen through slots; no single list of all meals.

Y · two-section win

Explicit library = one place to see/manage them all.

5 · Edit a meal with the day's target in view X

Tune lunch portions to hit 2100 kcal.

X · schedule-centric win

Edit the meal in its slot → the day's running total vs target moves right there.

Y · two-section

Edit in the library, divorced from the day; flip to schedule to see the day total.
