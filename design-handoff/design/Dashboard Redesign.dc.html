Bulgarian Split Squat
CompoundPushCustom
About this movement
A single-leg squat with the rear foot elevated on a bench. It builds unilateral quad and glute strength while challenging balance and hip stability — a strong choice for correcting side-to-side imbalances without heavy spinal loading.
Instructions
1
Set up in a split stance with the rear foot resting on a bench and the torso tall.
2
Lower under control until the front thigh is roughly parallel to the floor.
3
Drive through the front heel to stand, keeping the knee tracking over the toes.
Cues
Keep about 90% of the weight in the front leg.
Ribs down — avoid arching the lower back at the bottom.
A slight forward lean shifts the emphasis toward the glutes.
Target muscles
QuadsGlutesHamstrings
Equipment
DumbbellsFlat bench

NBNutrition · Plan builder — day-first flat list
Replaces the old two-pane builder. One primary column — the meals-library / days split is gone; you build the day you're looking at. Meals are a free list with a slot tag (no fixed 6-slot grid), the macro header is a compact kcal line (expandable), and alternates are per-meal "swaps" — the meal itself is the default.
Desktop
Fully interactive: switch / add / rename / delete days and re-assign weekdays; the energy line and macro meters compute live from the day’s meals; expand a meal to edit its items (picker + amount sheet), rename, re-slot, reorder, or remove it; manage swaps per meal. Meals reused across days show a "Used in N places" tag. Alternates live inside the meal ("Client can swap with"), so the meal itself is the default — no Default chips to manage. "Add meal" is live — it creates a new meal or reuses one that already exists in the plan’s other days.
